African Grey Parrot

Scientific Name: Psittacus erithacus

Physical Features: Medium-sized grey parrot with bright red tail feathers and black beak. Known for intelligent expression and sharp eyes.

Distribution: Rainforests of West and Central Africa

Habitat: Dense tropical rainforests and open forest edges

Food: Fruits, nuts, seeds, berries, and leafy matter

Social Behaviors: Highly intelligent and social; capable of mimicking human speech. Forms strong pair bonds. Nests in tree cavities; lays 3–5 eggs; incubation lasts 30 days

Life span: 40–60 years (can reach up to 80 in captivity)

Sulphur-Crested Cockatoo

Scientific Name: Cacatua galerita

Physical Features: Large white bird with plumed yellow crest and dark bill. Female has red-brown eyes and male darker brown. Approx. 50 cm in length.

Distribution: New Guinea, Eastern Australia, New Zealand

Habitat: Tropical and subtropical rainforests

Food: Seeds, buds, leaves, fruits, roots, tubers, bulbs

Social Behaviors: Geophagy (eating clay to detoxify food), breeding in May–Sept in tree hollows. 2–3 eggs, 25–27 days incubation

Life span: 40–80 years

Blue-and-Yellow Macaw

Scientific Name: Ara ararauna

Physical Features: Vivid with blue wings, golden underparts, green forehead, strong black beak, 33" from beak to tail, 40"+ wingspan

Distribution: Venezuela to Brazil (South America)

Habitat: Tropical forests, woodlands, savannas

Food: Seeds, nuts, fruits

Social Behaviors: Sexual maturity ~4 yrs, seasonal breeding, nests in high tree hollows. 2–3 eggs, 24–36 days incubation

Life span: 30–45 years
